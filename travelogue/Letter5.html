Jason Hutchens: letter five

Greetings all! Well, it's been an absolute age since I last wrote. Many months, in fact. Just long enough for me to have forgotten the intervening period, meaning that this letter will be a great deal shorter owing to lack of information.

When I last wrote I was about to head off to Lisbon in Portugal for a conference. And, of course, a holiday. Cathy, Tsung and D. were to join me for the weekend following the conclusion of the conference. But I had to get over there first, and that proved to be a good sight more complicated than I had envisioned.

It turns out that Portugal had a bit of a disagreement with Australia concerning the number of East Timorese refugees allowed into our country (East Timor being a former Portugese colony). They protested at this by requiring all Aussie visitors to their country to carry an entry visa. Australia reciprocated, of course, resulting in a stalemate. The upshot of all this is that innocent tourists are inconvenienced and nothing political is resolved.

Getting an entry visa is not normally a problem, but I needed to get one from the Portugese consulate in downtown Tel Aviv, and it turns out that they've never issued an entry visa to an Australian citizen before. They put it down as a learning experience, but from my point of view it was two weeks of frustration personified.

In the end it was all worth it. The conference was a rather lacklustre affair, but that is usually the case with conferences anyway so I didn't complain. The highlight was probably the taxi ride to the hotel from the airport. Taxi drivers around the world are generally insane, but Portugese taxi drivers really take the cake.
